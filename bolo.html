Kiki's Chocolate Cake


Movie: Kiki's Delivery Service, 1989

Cook: Madame


This cake was a surprise gift from Madame to Kiki, as a way of appreciation for her sympathy and help. This gift heavily touches Kiki, who was nearly brought to tears upon receiving it. This dessert would bring joy to everyone at our picnic.

Ingredients


    For the cake:


  1. 1 cups sugar
  2. 3/4 cup flour (plus 2 tbsp)
  3. 1/4 cup cocoa powder (plus 2 tbsp)
  4. 1 teaspoon baking soda
  5. 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  6. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  7. 1 egg
  8. 1/2 cup buttermilk
  9. 1/2 cup cooled coffee (I made a cup of Nescafe instant Colombian)
  10. 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  11. 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

    12. For the frosting:


  12. 1/2 bag mini semi-sweet chocolate chips (or regular-sized chips, finely chopped)
  13. 3/4 cup heavy whipping cream
  14. 4 tbsp salted butter

    15. For the decoration:


  15. 1 cup white frosting
  16. 1 green icing pen
  17. 1 red icing pen

Instructions


  1. You’ll actually want to start with your frosting, since it needs time to chill.

  2. First, place your chocolate chips in a large bowl and set aside.

  3. Cook the whipping cream and butter in a saucepan over medium heat until it starts boiling. Keep stirring! Pour the cream mix over the chocolate and whisk briskly until completely smooth.

  4. Place some plastic wrap over the bowl and put it in the fridge to chill. Wait 1 hour.

  5. Go watch Spirited Away or something. Then, stir the mix thoroughly. Replace the cover and put the mix back in the fridge for another hour or until it reaches spreading consistency.

  6. Now that the frosting is done and needs to chill, let's start the cake! Preheat your oven to 350°. Thoroughly spray a 9-inch round cake pan with cooking spray or grease it with butter and flour. This cake sticks a lot, so be sure to thoroughly coat every corner of it.

  7. Dump the sugar, flour, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, and salt into a large bowl. Whisk it!

  8. Whisk in the egg, buttermilk, coffee, oil and vanilla. Beat the mix briskly for 2 minutes.

  9. Pour the batter into your pan and bake for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  10. When fully baked, set the cake pan on a wire rack to cool for 10 minutes. Then flip the cake out of the pan and allow it to cool completely on the rack.

  11. When the cake is cool, spread on a layer of chocolate frosting. Let it set for 5-10 minutes.

  12. Use a toothpick to draw out where you’ll put the decorative icing. This makes it easier to replicate the look of the cake in the movie. If you make a mistake, just use an icing knife or butter knife to smooth it out.

  13. Pipe your decorative icing over the toothpick drawing and allow it to set for another 5-10 minutes.
